Agmenting Performance in Cyber Physical Space

The project explores augmenting performance in the cyber-physical space and is divided into two key parts: privacy for the audience and augmenting performance. Rhino.Inside is used to incorporate voxels generated by Grasshopper from the intersection of the cone of vision of the audience, which is then blurred in Unity, using Kawase blur and URP, a Unity plugin. Then, to augment the performance, an existing machine learning model, MediaPipe Pose, is modified to generate a skeleton of the performer by detecting the performer and forming 33 three-dimensional landmarks of the joints of the human body in real-time in Unity. Then, agent-based modelling (ABM), is used along with Perlin noise and an attractor to generate the augmented performance. The output is tested in HoloLens from the audience's perspective in different scenarios.
